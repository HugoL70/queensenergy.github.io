Introduction

This project investigates energy usage at Queens College to uncover patterns in heating, cooling, and temperature behavior across campus buildings. Our goal is to support sustainability initiatives and propose actionable recommendations.

Data Sources

  • Building management system data (temperature, flow, power)
  • Outside weather temperature data
  • Historical utility consumption logs

Key Findings

Based on the collected data:

  • Significant energy usage during early morning hours.
  • Outside temperature directly affects heating loads.
  • Periods of high volume flow correspond to peak demand events.
